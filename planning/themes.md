# Themes & Tone

## Core Themes

### Identity
The central question: if your body, your name, your relationships, and your daily life all belong to someone else — what remains of you? Alex's struggle is not just practical but ontological. Who is he when every external marker of "Alex" is gone?

### Ownership and Embodiment
Alex inhabits a body that is not his. This raises questions about autonomy, consent, and what it means to "be" someone. He is performing Yooyeon while being Alex — and the performance, over time, may begin to reshape the performer.

### The Gap Between Perception and Reality
Alex knew Yooyeon as a fan knows an idol: through image, performance, curated content. The story forces him to confront the distance between that and the real person whose life he now occupies. What he assumed and what is true will frequently diverge.

### Isolation Inside Intimacy
Alex is surrounded by people (the members) who think they know him (as Yooyeon). He is never alone, and yet he is completely alone. This tension — the loneliness of being misrecognized — is a recurring emotional note.

### Slow Change
Alex changes over the course of the story whether he intends to or not. So do his relationships with the members. The story is interested in gradual, nearly imperceptible shifts — the kind that only become visible in retrospect.

### Forced Growth
Alex's pre-swap life was organized around avoidance: keep the status quo, manage others out of closeness, survive without risking anything. He understood this pattern and could name it. The understanding changed nothing, because avoidance was always the easier option.

The swap closes that option by force. It is not a lesson or a persuasion. It is the removal of the architecture he used to avoid change. The story is interested in what happens to someone when the only way forward is the thing they have spent years not doing — not because they are now ready, but because the alternative is no longer available.

## Tone Notes

- **Grounded, not fantastical.** The body swap is the one impossible thing. Everything else follows real-world logic.
- **Measured, not dramatic.** Alex is 30. He processes slowly and imperfectly. His narration is often understated, sometimes even wry.
- **Emotionally honest.** His discomfort with his body, his situation, his feelings — none of it is tidied up or resolved quickly.
- **Quiet dread** is the emotional baseline, not panic. The horror (if any) is slow and existential, not sudden.

## Motifs

- Mirrors and reflections
- Names (being called a name that isn't yours)
- Muscle memory (the body knowing things Alex doesn't)
- Photographs (the record of someone who isn't there anymore)
- Voice (hearing yourself in a voice that isn't yours)

---

## What to Avoid

- Treating the swap as played for comedy (brief moments are fine, but it's not a farce)
- Solving the emotional problems too quickly
- Making the tripleS members flat or interchangeable
- Making Alex either too competent or too helpless — he muddles through, imperfectly
