# Subplots

## Framework

The arc structure in `outline.md` is the story's chronological spine — it tracks Alex's experience as a sequence of events. Subplots are the secondary lines of motion that give the spine volume: they branch off it, give it substance, and occasionally redirect it. Each arc should carry a subplot load it can hold; this document defines what those subplots are and how they function.

### Four subplot categories for this story

**Thematic mirror subplots**
Another character's arc that echoes Alex's themes from a different angle — identity, belonging, conditional care. The reader watches Alex struggle with X while a member struggles with a different flavor of the same X. The mirror makes both richer. These are arc-length subplots spanning multiple arcs.

**Relational subplots**
The ongoing evolution of specific member-to-member relationships, shaped (often indirectly) by Alex's presence. Alex doesn't have to be the focus of these — but his being there changes the conditions under which members relate to each other.

**Practical pressure subplots**
The external forces of idol life that create situational pressure on Alex's performance — schedules, unit preparation, fan events, content obligations. These provide the friction and near-discovery moments that keep the story grounded.

**Investigation subplots**
Alex trying to understand what happened — to him, to Yooyeon, to the question of reversal.

## Ensemble Tier Structure

24 members is too many for equal treatment in a single-POV story. Subplot decisions must be made within an explicit tier structure to prevent drift — accidentally over-committing to a minor character whose thread then requires weight it was never given.

| Tier | Count | Treatment |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Arc-level characters (full subplot arcs) | 4–5 | Kotone, Mayu, Seoyeon — 1–2 others TBD |
| Scene-presence characters (recurring texture, no full arc) | 8–10 | Nakyoung, Yeonji, Hayeon, Jiwoo, Hyerin, others |
| Background members (group scenes only) | Remaining | Present, named, not developed individually |

Before writing any scene that gives a minor character emotional weight, check their tier. Tier reassignment requires explicit decision.

## POV Strategy

The story is close first-person (Alex's voice, his interiority). Seoyeon's arc before and during the reveal may use selective POV shifts when necessary. Three options remain available:

- **Third-person limited for Seoyeon sections** — most readable; her sections feel tonally distinct from Alex's by design, which can do work
- **First-person shift** — "I" becomes Seoyeon for those sections; riskier, requires very clean section breaks to prevent disorientation
- **Remain in Alex's POV, go deeper on observation** — not a true shift, but close observation of Seoyeon through what Alex notices (her face, body, silences, what she asks and doesn't) can carry significant interiority

Decision deferred. To be confirmed when the first Seoyeon scene requiring a shift is written.

## Structural Principle: Each scene does double duty

In a slow-burn single-POV story, every scene should advance at least two things simultaneously: the main arc and a relationship or subplot. This prevents internal monologue from becoming static and keeps the story moving even in quiet sections. A scene that serves only one purpose is a scene that can be compressed.

## Confirmed Subplots

### 1. The Yooyeon Trail
**Type:** Investigation
**Arc span:** Arc 2 (core), threads into Arc 3 and forward
**Summary:** Alex, alone in Yooyeon's apartment during Arc 2, begins piecing together who she actually was. The sources: her phone, laptop or tablet, personal objects, messages, photos, private correspondence, whatever her space contains. The investigation continues into Arc 3 through what he learns from how the members speak about her and to her. The trail runs alongside the dream sequences (see subplot 3): what he learns about her in waking life and what he perceives of her in dreams are two separate and sometimes contradictory pictures of the same person.

The trail has a built-in emotional complication: the more complete the picture becomes, the heavier the knowledge of what he displaced. She is not the Yooyeon he knew from YouTube. Every piece of evidence closes the distance between the public image and the real person — and closing that distance makes the displacement harder, not easier, to carry.

**Scope to cover:** Family relationships and dynamics (including the university pressure context — see characters/yooyeon.md); friendships outside the group; the idol world as she experienced it from the inside; private habits, objects, and details Alex did not expect; anything that diverges sharply from the public image Alex held before the swap.

**Structural function:** Investigation subplot; deepens Yooyeon's absent-presence throughout the story; feeds directly into the ending (he has built a near-complete picture of her by the time the dreams stop).

### 2. The Identity Leak
**Type:** Practical pressure / narrative
**Arc span:** Day 0 onward — present from the first scene Alex is in front of the group, building through Arcs 3–4, until Kotone pieces it together
**Summary:** Alex makes small decisions as Yooyeon that the real Yooyeon would not have made. Not dramatic slips — texture. A different reaction to something. A silence where Yooyeon would have spoken. An answer that is almost right but not quite. A look that doesn't match. Members sense something slightly off without being able to name it. The accumulation is what Kotone eventually reads correctly — not any single incident, but a pattern that becomes undeniable only in retrospect.

The leak is gradual by design. It cannot be a noticeable performance failure in the early arcs — at that point it registers only as something being slightly different about Yooyeon today. It becomes legible as a pattern only over weeks of contact, and only to someone who looks past performance to the person underneath.

**Structural function:** Practical pressure / narrative subplot; creates sustained low-grade dramatic irony across Arcs 2–4; feeds directly into Kotone's discovery.

### 3. The Dream Thread (Third Mechanism)
**Type:** Investigation / thematic
**Arc span:** Arc 2 onward, recurring through Arc 5; the dreams stop when the return mechanism is activated
**Summary:** Alex sees Yooyeon in his dreams — or she appears there. The contact evolves across the story's arc. In early dreams she is present but not reachable: visible, unclear, the gap uncloseable from his side. Over time, something shifts. The dreams become genuine, if partial, interaction — not fully legible conversation, but real contact: ethereal, dreamlike, the texture of two people in the same space who can't quite hold the same frame at once.

Alex begins narrating to her. Not knowing whether she hears, not receiving clean confirmation, but choosing to tell her things — significant events, moments in her life he has lived in her absence. Comebacks, schedules, the texture of her relationships. He treats her as someone who deserves to know what is happening in her own life. The narration is not performed; it is a private choice, made in the space the dreams provide. It is also, though he cannot see it this way yet, the first form of care he gives freely and without calculation.

These sequences are not frequent or melodramatic. They are recurring, brief, and emotionally precise. They do double work: fueling the Yooyeon trail (what he learns about her in waking life and what he perceives of her in dreams are two separate and sometimes contradictory pictures of the same person) and building toward the ending (the final dream is structurally distinct from all that preceded it — see Arc 6 documentation in outline.md).

The reader, knowing Yooyeon's backstory — the university pressure, the exhaustion of balancing that with the idol career — may understand what she is experiencing in the dream state better than Alex does. He is reading fragments of her. The reader can infer more.

**Dream thread evolution by arc:**
- Arc 2: First appearances. She is present but unreachable. He reaches; the gap does not close. He wakes carrying something he can't name. Disorienting, unresolved.
- Arc 3–4: Contact becomes more consistent. He learns to wait rather than reach. He begins speaking to her without expecting response — telling her things. A relationship forms in adverse conditions, partial and wordless, but real.
- Arc 5: The contact deepens as the story's pressures peak. She is more present, more specific. The near-contact quality intensifies. He has told her enough by now that she has a picture — not complete, but real — of who he is and what he has built.
- Arc 6: The final dream is categorically different. The gap closes from her side. Something is communicated that has not been communicated before. Then the dreams stop.

**Key open question:** In the moments when Alex almost reaches her — when the gap almost closes — does she reach back, or does she hold the distance? This answer defines her character across the story's full arc. The answer should be consistent with the ending branch being written (see endings/ branches for branch-specific resolution of this question).

**Structural function:** Primary vehicle for Yooyeon's presence in the story; investigation subplot; direct feed into the ending regardless of branch.

### 4. Kotone-Yeonji
**Type:** Relational
**Arc span:** Arc 3–4 (core), resolves before or during the group meeting
**Summary:** Kotone knows the truth. She watches Yeonji — her closest person in the group, someone with a warm and real relationship with Yooyeon — extend genuine warmth to Alex-as-Yooyeon. The warmth Yeonji offers is the same patient generosity she once gave the real Yooyeon. Kotone is managing a secret that directly concerns someone she loves, while watching that person's warmth land on the wrong recipient.

The subplot hinges on one decision Kotone carries across this period: does she protect the secret even at the cost of watching it happen — possibly because the closeness to Yeonji makes her more protective, not wanting to hurt her — or does something push her toward eventually telling Yeonji the truth before the group meeting?

The stakes of each option: if Yeonji finds out at the group meeting with everyone else, she has been unknowingly lied to by her closest person through the entire preceding period. If Kotone tells her before, Yeonji becomes complicit in the secret and carries it into the meeting already knowing. Neither outcome is clean.

**Open:** Which path does Kotone take? What, if anything, makes the decision for her?

**Structural function:** Relational subplot; deepens Kotone's guilt arc; raises the real cost of secret-keeping beyond just Alex's survival.

### 5. The Member's Personal Crisis
**Type:** Thematic mirror
**Arc span:** Arc 3 or 5 (TBD — depends on which member and what crisis)
**Summary:** One member is going through something personal that has nothing to do with Alex — and Alex (as Yooyeon) is expected to be the person they bring it to. Yooyeon's door was always open; it is still open, and the member doesn't know she's not home. Alex cannot retreat. He has to be present for someone else's pain, in a capacity he has never occupied and never practiced.

This tests his architecture in a register nothing else does. Not the pressure of discovery, not the weight of Seoyeon, not the assault. Just the demand of being the person someone needs — when everything in him defaults to managing people out of closeness, not deeper into it.

The crisis should rhyme with one of Alex's themes without duplicating it. Candidates: a member privately uncertain whether she wants to stay in the group; a family situation she hasn't told anyone about; something about her identity outside the idol frame; the weight of public performance against a private self that doesn't match it.

**Open:** Who is the member, and what is the crisis? Must be someone in Alex's immediate orbit (Spring haus or close contact). The crisis must rhyme thematically with Alex's situation without being a direct mirror.

**Structural function:** Thematic mirror subplot; forces Alex into a relational demand his pre-swap life never made of him; one of the earliest tests of whether the architecture can be breached from the outside.

---

### 6. Seoyeon Before the Reveal
**Type:** Relational
**Arc span:** Arc 3 — runs until the confrontation scene
**Summary:** What is Seoyeon's internal experience during the weeks when something feels wrong with Yooyeon but she doesn't know why? She has been asking — has had at least one conversation where she asked Yooyeon directly what is wrong. She arrives at each interaction with Alex already carrying accumulated suspicion and unresolved feeling. Alex observes her from the inside of the life she's trying to reach, without the context of the romantic history that would make her behavior legible.

This subplot is told from Alex's POV through deep observation. He is reading a person he doesn't know, using the wrong frame. The reader, who has more context than Alex, understands Seoyeon's behavior better than he does — which creates a dramatic irony that runs until the confrontation.

**What to show:** How Seoyeon tests the situation. What she watches for. The conversations that almost surface the truth before the reveal does. What she's carrying that Alex can see but can't name.

**Structural function:** Relational subplot; builds toward the confrontation scene with full emotional weight; establishes what Seoyeon is carrying so that Alex's flat delivery of the truth in the confrontation lands with the damage it should.

## Arc-by-Arc Subplot Load

### Arc 0 — Last Week
No ensemble subplots available. Alex hasn't entered that world. One task: plant something in his ordinary life that will resonate later — an unanswered call, an object, a thought — that the story can return to. Thin by design, not empty.

### Arc 1 — The Swap
Contained and claustrophobic by design. Dream thread begins here — the first appearance, disorienting and unresolved. One background pressure to maintain: Alex's real life does not pause. His phone, his job, his one friend — what is happening to that life in real time? This thread doesn't need scenes of its own yet, just presence.

### Arc 2 — First Steps
Richest arc for investigation subplots. Yooyeon trail (core). Dream thread (first recurring appearances). Identity leak begins (Alex alone with her space, then first near-misses with people who know her — where the leak starts to register).

### Arc 3 — The Others
Widest arc for character space. Practical pressure subplots active (Visionary Vision preparation, group schedules, fan events). Identity leak accumulates. Kotone-Yeonji subplot begins. Member's personal crisis if placed in Arc 3. Seoyeon before the reveal running through the whole arc. Dream thread continues.

### Arc 4 — The Fracture
Identity leak reaches its conclusion (Kotone's discovery). Reveal structure unfolds (Stages 1–5 per outline.md). Kotone-Yeonji subplot resolves. Dream thread continues — may intensify during or after the reveal as pressure on Alex increases.

### Arc 5 — Slow Burn
Relational subplots pay off: Seoyeon's arc, Kotone's guilt, Nakyoung question, Mayu-Alex dynamic, assault scene and its ripple. Member's personal crisis if placed here. Dream thread continues, intensifying as the story's pressures peak.

**The unspoken goodbye window** opens in the latter part of Arc 5, after Alex discovers the return mechanism but before he activates it. He does not tell anyone — not the members, not Yooyeon in the dream space (withholding from her specifically to prevent her from acting first). In the window between discovery and activation, he moves toward certain people in ways he has not before. Not dramatic gestures. Something he had not done with that person previously — a question he had not asked, a room he stays in longer, a moment where he listens instead of managing the distance. The members do not know what they are receiving. He knows exactly what he is giving. At least one of these attempts does not land — the person isn't ready to receive it — and he carries that without trying again. The incomplete thing is part of what he accepts. Specific members this window touches: to be determined by which relationships most need a quiet note before the ending. Seoyeon and Sohyun are specifically identified as the more resistant members toward whom he makes an attempt — not to extract resolution, but to see them once as real people rather than as positions they hold toward him.

### Arc 6 — Endings
Subplots should close before or within the ending, or resolve differently per branch. The dream thread's end is the structural trigger for the bittersweet ending.
