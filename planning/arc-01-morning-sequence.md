## Arc 1 Scope and Pacing Constraint

**Arc 1 runs from Day 0 (June 24, 2024) through approximately the Visionary Vision gravity

What Arc 1 does NOT contain:
- Any idol stage performance
- Any serious choreography confrontation

What Arc 1 IS:
- Alex alone with the body
- Alex alone with the apartment

**Glow** (Chaewon, Sullin, SeoAh, Jiyeon) is the active unit as of Day 0 — their promotion
is ongoing. This is not Alex's unit. That is part of why he has a buffer.

## The Wake — Before the Room Comes Alive

### Why he wakes at ~5:30 AM

Alex's 5:30 AM wake time is habit-driven, not body-driven. The low-grade vigilance that has
gotten him out of bed every workday for years fires at its usual time — the anxiety of being
late doesn't know the job is gone.

But Yooyeon's body has not been trained to 5:30 AM. Idol schedules run later. The body is
**heavy** — groggy in a way his own body was not at this hour. His mental alert system fires;
the body resists. That is the first collision between his mind and the new body, and it arrives
before he is conscious enough to understand why everything else is wrong.

Additional layer: Alex's own body was already accumulating the effects of bad habits —
poor diet, no real exercise, some weight beginning to form, sometimes waking not fully rested
or with something aching. Yooyeon's body is younger, lighter, presumably better maintained.
The heaviness he feels is specifically about the hour, not about the body's condition. He will
notice the contrast later — the difference between this body's actual baseline and what he
expected from waking up feeling this way.

## The Discovery Sequence

Slow. Natural. One thing at a time.

### Stage 1 — The ceiling

Consciousness returning. Eyes open. The ceiling is wrong — not his ceiling, different texture,
different light, different height. Look around: the walls, curtains, window, the objects in the
room. None of it matches his apartment.

> *[WRITE: establish the specific quality of Yooyeon's room — colors, objects, light coming
> through the curtains at this hour. This is the first scene where the Yooyeon trail begins,
> though he doesn't know that yet. What he sees here is what she left behind. Keep the
> inventory brief but specific — one or two things that are hers in a way that hits later.]*

### Stage 2 — Sitting up

The wrongness of the room forces a rude movement — sitting up fast, disoriented. This is
when the **hair falls**.

For someone who has never had long hair: the sensation of it falling across shoulders and
into the face is disorienting before it is understood. It arrives as sensation first, not as
information. He doesn't immediately know what it means — only that something moved that
shouldn't have.

> *[WRITE: the hair sensation in physical/tactile terms, not explained. He reacts to it
> before he names it. One beat.]*

### Stage 3 — Hands, arms, chest

Looking down: the hands are wrong. The arms are wrong. The chest is wrong — not what he
expects, and also different pajamas. The **slenderness** of this body. The proportions don't
match.

> *[WRITE: Alex's INTP pattern fires here — he is already assembling evidence before he
> is ready to hear the conclusion. This is not panicked inventory. This is the analytical
> layer trying to categorize what it is seeing before the emotional layer catches up.]*

### Stage 4 — Getting up and falling

Panic overrides the analytical layer. He tries to get up. The center of gravity is wrong:
this body is shorter, lighter, differently balanced than his own. His muscle memory carries
a different body's geometry and it fails him.

**Craft note (2026-04-18):** Full fall and stumble-and-catch are both defensible. The scene
should render the gap between *intention* and *reality* — not the physics. He intends to stand;
the body doesn't execute it the way his muscle memory expects. The stumble-and-catch may fit
Alex's non-theatrical register better than a full fall: hand finding the bedframe, one knee
down, the body catching itself *without him directing it*. That instinctive catch is its own
kind of wrongness — the body has reflexes he didn't trigger.

From the catch: the feet are wrong. Smaller. The legs are slender.

> *[WRITE: ungraceful, quick, no slow-motion. He doesn't stay down. He's already moving
> toward the mirror. Option: keep as full fall per original instinct, or use stumble-and-catch
> — decide when writing the scene.]*

### Stage 5 — The deduction

Alex does not need to reach for the obvious confirmation. He is an INTP. He has assembled:
the hair, the hands, the chest, the slender limbs, the different body proportions. He knows
what this means before the mirror tells him. The mirror is not where the deduction happens —
the deduction is already done.

The mirror is where the **weight** of it lands.

### Stage 6 — The mirror

He goes to it because he has to see it confirmed in full. Not because he doesn't know — he
knows. Because there is a difference between knowing and standing in front of it.

**The mirror has two beats, not one.**

*First beat:* Not his face. A stranger. He looks; the face does not change. He may move —
lean closer, shift angle — the body moves with him in the mirror. Confirmation comes through
repeated motion, not the first look.

*Second beat:* Recognition. After a beat — seconds — the face becomes placed. He has seen
this face before. Scrolling, YouTube, social media. A group. tripleS. One of the members.
Her. *Yooyeon.* That delay between "stranger" and "I know who this is" is its own register.
A stranger's face would be abstract. A real person's face — a public figure, someone with
23 people who know her — makes the situation specific and concrete in a way that lands harder.
Recognition makes the mirror worse, not better.

**First breakdown here.** The emotional wall the analytical layer has been holding since 5:30
breaks at the recognition, not at the first look. This is Alex: not theatrical, not loud. The
breakdown is real. It looks like going still.

The shape of it: *Why am I a woman?* But underneath that, already: *Why am I her?*

**The shame/curiosity compound enters here.** He has had private pre-swap moments of
imagining inhabiting a different body — sometimes female, in the daydream register of wanting
to be someone else entirely. The reality is nothing like the imagining. The imagining is now
in the room with him regardless. The gap between what he fantasized and what this actually
is carries shame that exists independent of whether the connection is rational.

> *[WRITE: do not name the fantasy explicitly in this scene. The shame is present without
> its source being stated — the reader has it from Arc 0. In Arc 1, it is just an unreasonable
> shame with no visible explanation. That is exactly how he would experience it.]*

**The spiral that follows.** Alex's breakdown pattern is stillness: he goes quiet, goes minimal,
blood pressure climbs, waits for it to pass. This breakdown, in this body, may produce crying
before his architecture can intercept it — the hormonal environment delivering something faster
than he can stop. If it arrives, it is not chosen. The body does it before he decides to. He
comes back to the edge of the bed. Hands occupied with something (her room, not his — no
Rubik's cube, nothing familiar). Eyes not tracking. Thoughts running with nowhere to go.

**Whether he cries is a structural choice with consequences for the Mayu knock:**
- If he does cry, Mayu notices red eyes and an unsteady voice when she knocks. She files it
  as *something was wrong with her this morning*. This is the first entry in the identity leak
  — Day 0, before any pattern has had time to accumulate. She doesn't know what she's filing.
- If he doesn't cry, the first Mayu interaction starts clean. The identity leak begins later.

> *[DECIDE: does he cry? This affects the texture of every subsequent interaction that
> morning and whether the identity leak has a Day 0 entry.]*

### Stage 7 — The bathroom

The first breakdown is interrupted by the practical: he has to use the bathroom.

This is the first full confrontation with the body's reality in a register the mirror doesn't
produce. The mirror shows him what she looks like. The bathroom shows him what she is.

> *[WRITE: write from inside his consciousness — not looking at the body, inhabiting it.
> He reaches for something familiar and it isn't there. The body's presence forces something
> on him that the fantasy never prepared him for. Do not name the fantasy in scene — his
> expectation simply fails, and that failure IS the gap. Brief. Then cut. The aftermath
> (what he understands sitting down after, what he now knows about vulnerability) matters
> more than the moment itself. One paragraph maximum, probably less.]*

## The Phone

Established: Alex picks up the phone after the mirror, not before.

He now knows who he is — he recognized the face. The phone confirms it: her face on the
lock screen, probably her name on waiting notifications.

The question is whether he can open it. Two options:
- He unlocks it: her schedule, her contacts, a message already waiting. The practical crisis
  asserts itself. Being forced into the logistics may be what ends the spiral — function
  as a return mechanism. The world asks him to be her, and the asking pulls him back.
- He picks it up, sees her face, and puts it down. Too much. Not yet. He is not ready to
  read the life he has just been handed.

> *[DECIDE: does he open the phone in Arc 1, or does that wait for Arc 2? If he opens it
> in Arc 1, what does he see — a specific message, a schedule entry, something from a member?
> If he puts it down, the phone becomes the object he can't look at yet, which has its own
> texture during the spiral.]*

**Note on the shower:** The deeper reckoning with the body — the first full confrontation
with what it is in a sustained, unavoidable way — is placed in Arc 2, not Arc 1. The bathroom
moment in Arc 1 is functional and brief. The shower comes later and carries the weight.

## The Voice Test

At some point in the 1-2 hours of private time, the functional layer reasserts. He wants to
know what the voice sounds like before he has to produce it for someone. Not dramatic — one
syllable, a cleared throat, a word read from something in the room. Brief, clinical, just
checking the variable.

The test tells him what the voice sounds like alone, in a quiet room. It does not prepare him
for using it.

## The Haus Comes Alive — First External Demand

Alex has ~1-2 hours alone before anyone in the haus is likely awake. He uses that time
imperfectly — the disorientation is too acute for real processing, but it is the only private
window he gets.

**Someone knocks to ask about breakfast.** Established candidate: **Mayu**.

The exchange happens **through a closed door.** She has not seen him yet. The door muffles
the voice, gives him cover he didn't arrange. His one job: confirm he's awake and answer the
eggs question.

**The line (established):**
*"Yooyeon unnie, are you awake? I'm making breakfast, I want to make some eggs, are you
ok with that?"*

**"Yooyeon unnie"** carries: the name, the gender, the relationship, the hierarchical position
(older-sister, the person whose door is always open) — all in one address. It places him in a
social structure he has never occupied. He will be handed this word again, from many people.
It will never stop being slightly wrong.

**What he produces:** He can get the content right — yes, awake, eggs are fine. He cannot
easily get the texture right. Yooyeon would add warmth: a thanks, some small softness that
fits how she relates to Mayu. He confirms. He doesn't add anything.

Mayu gets the right answer in the wrong register. Not wrong enough to name. Slightly flat,
slightly short. She files it without knowing she's filing it.

**This is the first identity leak entry — Day 0, before any pattern has accumulated.** No
visible evidence. Nothing she can act on. Just a texture that doesn't quite fit.

Neither Alex nor Mayu knows the weight of this exchange. She is just making breakfast.
He is just getting through the next thirty seconds.

> determines whether he has to navigate more than one interaction before the day is over.]*

> *[WRITE: the moment before he answers — the pause, then the answer. Short. Then the
> sound of her going back to the kitchen. Then the silence of having survived it.]*

## Going to Breakfast

**Established: he goes.** Not because he's ready. Because not going costs more.

The cost calculation: staying requires a convincing excuse through the door, Mayu's care
reflex already slightly activated, possible further check-ins during the day. Going requires
appearing, sitting, eating eggs, producing minimum-viable Yooyeon for the duration of
breakfast. The numbers are not close.

His INTP profile also pulls toward information-gathering: the kitchen gives him data he
needs. Who else is in the haus. What a normal morning here looks like. What is expected of
Yooyeon in this space. He doesn't frame it as intelligence-gathering. He just goes.

The spiral is not resolved when he gets up — it is on hold while the functional mode runs.
He sits, eats, answers with the minimum required. The performance is low-intensity. He
survives it.

**Craft note:** The breakfast scene does not need to be long. A few paragraphs. He appears,
sits, the morning goes through its motions, and it ends. The weight is not in what happens
at the table — it's in the fact that he got himself there before he was ready, and that this
is going to keep happening.

> *[WRITE: who is at the table, what small exchange occurs, what he produces. What Mayu
> notices or doesn't. The moment it's over and he can stop.]*

## The Phone — Established Sequence

**Trigger:** At breakfast, someone asks if Yooyeon has anything scheduled today. Alex says
he needs to check — natural, not suspicious. This gets him to the phone without it being a
deliberate dramatic beat.

**Touch ID:** The phone unlocks on his thumb. Her biometric data is his now. He didn't
break in — he was simply recognized. A quiet beat: the first time the body does something
useful for him, which is strange to sit with.

**What he finds:** Calendar mostly clear. One note toward the end of the week — something
about Visionary Vision, a Gravity announcement.

**The knowledge gap:** He does not fully know what "Gravity" means in the tripleS context.
He has a vague sense that fans vote for sub-unit selections — casual YouTube knowledge — but
the specific term and mechanics are fan-level detail he doesn't have.

**He does not ask.** His INTP risk-assessment fires immediately: asking "what's a Gravity"
to someone who has lived inside this system for years is exactly the question that produces
"why don't you know that?" He identifies the gap, identifies that surfacing it is dangerous,
and files it for private research.

**His answer at the table:** "Nothing today." True. Sufficient. The end-of-week note stays
in his pocket.

This is the first instance of a pattern that runs through the whole pre-reveal arc: gap in
knowledge of her world → assess that asking would expose him → add to private research
agenda. The Yooyeon trail begins here, before he's even alone with her belongings.
