# Naming Conventions — How Members Address Yooyeon

> Reference for writing dialogue and internal narration across all arcs. Each form carries
> a specific relational weight. Alex must learn to respond to all of them as himself.
> Established in session 2026-04-20.

---

## Base Forms

| Form | Pronunciation guide | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| **Yooyeon unnie** | Yoo-yeon un-ni | Full name + honorific. Standard respectful form for younger members. |
| **Yooyeon-a** | Yoo-yeon-ah | Direct address particle (-a attaches to names ending in a consonant). Same-age or special closeness marker. Seoyeon's form exclusively — see below. |
| **Unnie** alone | Un-ni | No name attached. Only works when intimacy is high enough that it's clear which unnie. |
| **Yooyeon-i** | Yoo-yeon-ee | The -i particle softens the name, makes it more familiar and affectionate. |
| **Yooyeonni** | Yoo-yeon-ni | Natural fast-spoken contraction of "Yooyeon unnie." The haus-level shortcut. |
| **Yuyu** | Yoo-yoo | Doubling the first syllable. Cutesy and playful. Reserved for one specific dynamic. |
| **Yuni** | Yoo-ni | Compressed nickname — "Yoo" from Yooyeon + "ni" from unnie. Compact and casual. |
| **Yeonni** | Yeon-ni | Second syllable "yeon" + "ni" from unnie. Softer compression than Yooyeonni. |

## By Register

### Formal / full form — Yooyeon unnie

Warm but measured. The full form is not coldness — it is care expressed through correctness.

- **Mayu** — always the full Yooyeon unnie, reliable, unhurried. She doesn't shorten things.
- **Kotone** — full form sincerely in most contexts; in casual moments (e.g., walking in mid-motion) may drop to just "unnie"
- **Jiwoo** — Yooyeon unnie; her INFP quietness expressed through the complete form
- **Xinyu, Sullin** — full form as default; precision in an adopted language tends toward the standard
- **Foreign members generally** — full form unless specifically established otherwise

### Casual warm — Yooyeonni or Yooyeon-i

Comfortable enough to compress; not theatrical enough for full nicknames.

- **Nakyoung** — "unnie" alone in close moments; Yooyeonni in general. Doesn't need the full name. The shortcut is intimacy, not laziness.
- **Hyerin** — Yooyeonni; ESTP comfort with shortcuts, no ceremony
- **Dahyun, Yeonji** — Yooyeon-i or Yooyeonni; ally haus warmth in post-reveal arcs

### Playful / affectionate nicknames

High-energy members who turn names into something endearing.

- **Hayeon** — **Yuyu**. Almost certainly hers alone. One person coined it; nobody else would say it the same way.
- **Nien or Lynn** — one of them likely uses **Yuni**; to be confirmed when their scenes are written
- **Kaede** — possibly **Yooyeon-i unnie** (full -i + unnie); her warmth expressed through slight lengthening rather than compression

## Special Case — Seoyeon: Yooyeon-a

Same-age address form, used despite the unnie dynamic.

What it carries: *I am not placing you above me the way others do. You are mine in a different way.*

This form marks their co-leader relationship, their shared history, and the specific intimacy between them that exists outside the normal hierarchy. It is the only form in the group that bypasses the honorific entirely without being disrespectful — because it signals equivalence, not casualness.

**For Alex:** When Seoyeon calls him Yooyeon-a for the first time, it will be the address that lands most differently of all. It implies years of shared history he has no access to. He will not know why it hits the way it does. He will only know that it does.

---

## What This Means for Alex

He must learn to respond to all of these as himself. The practical consequences by form:

- **Yooyeon unnie** from Mayu — the fraction of a second the full form provides is real. He can orient before he has to respond.
- **Unnie alone** from Nakyoung — no buffer. One word, and he has to know it's him, right now.
- **Yuyu** from Hayeon — the first time it arrives, it will cost him a beat. Nothing in his prior life maps to being called that.
- **Yooyeon-a** from Seoyeon — hits differently than all others, even before he knows why. This form is the one that most obviously doesn't fit him.

The variety is also a practical problem: he cannot miss his cue because the form was abbreviated. "Unnie" in a room with six people could be addressed to anyone. He has to learn which unnie he is, in every context, without being taught.

---

## Formal vs. Informal Contexts

In managed settings (in front of cameras, management, public-facing content): members default to the fuller forms. In private haus settings, shortcuts and nicknames emerge. The naming conventions above apply primarily to private/haus contexts.
