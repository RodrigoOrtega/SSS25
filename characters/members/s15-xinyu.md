### Relationships

- **Sohyun (best friends, real feelings):** Best friends. They perform as a married couple in fanservice contexts — Xinyu's side of this is not performance. She genuinely likes Sohyun. The public expressions of affection, the married couple framing, the way she acts toward her — all of it is real feeling channeled through a form that has plausible deniability. She has not told Sohyun how she actually feels. Her reasoning is simple: she does not want to scare Sohyun away if Sohyun doesn't feel the same. The friendship is too important to risk. So the fanservice becomes the channel — the only form in which she can express what she feels without requiring an answer, because it is officially just performance. She is not aware that this arrangement also means she will never find out. She is not thinking about the ceiling; she is thinking about not losing what she has.
- **Nien (friends, language):** They bonded through shared language — both speak Mandarin, and were introduced to the group in close succession (S15, S13). The friendship has the specific comfort of being able to speak naturally, without managing register or translation. TBD what else has grown around that foundation.

## Thematic Note

Xinyu's situation is structurally parallel to Jiwoo's — a real feeling, undeclared, managed to protect the friendship — but through the opposite method. Jiwoo suppresses and withdraws; she gives less of herself to keep things safe. Xinyu expresses through performance; she gives more of herself, but in a form that can always be dismissed as not-real. Both are protecting the friendship. Both are also, by different routes, ensuring the feeling stays unanswered. Xinyu's version is arguably more generous — she gives Sohyun something real even if Sohyun can't receive it as such — but it is also the one that can go on indefinitely without a resolution point.
