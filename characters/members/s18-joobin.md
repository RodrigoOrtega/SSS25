## Profile

- **MBTI:** ISFJ — Responsible, conscientious, attentive to others' needs; fulfills obligations accurately; notices what people require before they ask. The observer quality fits: she watches, she catalogues, she does not broadcast what she's taken in.
- **Personality:** Members have called her the one with the highest mental age in the group. Her nickname is "Boss Baby" — she is among the youngest by birth year but presents with unusual composure and restraint. She maintains a poker face habitually. She does not over-perform warmth or fill space. In hosting contexts she has been described as speaking "concisely and logically like an announcer" — that is a description of precision and restraint, not charm. She is dry rather than loud; her humor register is deadpan. A documented quirk: ventriloquism. The asymmetries are humanizing — she is composed in social situations and genuinely reactive to fear. She cried during the Badge War haunted house challenge. She enjoys amusement park rides.

- **The observer.** The most structurally significant thing about Joobin for this story is not her intelligence or her English — it is JooManSa (주만사, *People Who JooBin Met*), her own SIGNAL sub-series launched July 2024. She interviews members, but she opens each episode with Daily Observation Notes she has prepared in advance about the guest. She watches people, she files what she notices, and she has built a format around it. She photographs others, not herself. She is wired to notice when something is different about a person — not because she is suspicious but because cataloguing people is simply what she does. This is load-bearing for Arc 3.

- **The "daughter" dynamic (Familyz):** The group treats her as the daughter of Yubin (S8) and Jiwoo (S3). The label originated in fandom from the syllabic echo in her name and a physical resemblance to both that the group finds funny. It was acknowledged by Joobin on fromm, then by all three in SIGNAL content — it is now official group material. Her real father attended the A Live 25 concert and insisted on taking a family photo with Jiwoo and Yubin. She accepted the bit and has continued accepting it. She receives the affection with equanimity — she is not flustered by it, she does not push it away. It is the one context where she is firmly cast as the youngest and leans into it without resistance.

- **Interests:** Photography — she photographs the people around her, not herself. Cooking — she had no cooking ability when she joined and built it gradually alongside Soomin; it is a developed skill rather than an innate one. Food more generally: brunch, bread, rice cakes, pasta, tteokbokki, fruits, desserts, milk tea. Amusement park rides. Handcrafting. Music. Favorite characters skew soft and affectionate — Kakao Friends' Ryan and Choonsik, Care Bears, Disney. Sweet-scented perfumes.

### Relationships

- **Hayeon (S19 — Tom & Jerry, pre-debut):** The bilateral relationship with the most documented texture. Joobin coined the name herself — Hayeon plays Tom, Joobin plays Jerry. They trained together at FLAT9 Dance & Vocal Academy before debut; the dynamic predates tripleS. Their register is bickering and playful antagonism. The Badge War moment is the clearest window into how it actually operates: Hayeon outmaneuvered Joobin at Geumneung Beach for a badge, Joobin cried. The naming is accurate — she is the reactive, outmaneuvered party against Hayeon's more active energy. They share Zenith orbital unit. The fanservice Joobin doesn't initiate, Hayeon occasionally produces regardless; the Tom & Jerry frame is probably how Joobin navigates it.

- **Yubin (S8) + Jiwoo (S3) — Familyz:** See above. Being placed in the same Zenith orbital unit as both means the dynamic operates in a daily structure, not just in occasional content.

- **Soomin (S6 — domestic, cooking):** They lived in the same room (Bingsu room) and learned baking together from scratch. Joobin had no cooking experience; the skill was built in that domestic context alongside Soomin. A quiet, low-key bond — not built through performance or scheduled content but through shared daily life.

- **Yeonji (S12 — school grade):** Despite Yeonji being born a year earlier (2008), Joobin started school early and they share the same school grade. Both were high school freshmen in 2024. This creates a concrete shared daily context outside of group schedules. They are both in the No Same Age Line.

- **Jiwoo (S3 — English):** Beyond the Familyz dynamic, they have a specific code-switching register — Jiwoo has some English and Joobin is fluent, so their interactions in content include Korean-English back-and-forth that is documented as a recognizable dynamic.

- **Sullin (S22 — translator, roommates):** Joobin is Sullin's primary English translator in group content. They live together in the BingSooOnDa room. The practical role is real; whether the relationship has built into something beyond utility is not documented.

- **Xinyu (S15 — admiration):** Joobin has stated she wants to get closer to Xinyu and finds her "tall and pretty." This appears to be largely one-directional admiration without a reciprocal documented dynamic.

- **Yooyeon (S5 — No Same Age Line, thin):** They are both part of the No Same Age Line — the named friendship of the four members who are each the only one born in their respective year (Yooyeon 2001, Yeonji 2008, Joobin 2009, SeoAh 2010). The structural connection is real. The relationship in practice is thin: Joobin joined later, their schedules rarely overlapped, the group's closer pairings had already formed around other axes by the time Joobin was integrated. They are not distant or cold — there is no specific tension. They simply haven't had much time alone together. The recognition of sharing the "alone in your year" quality exists; it hasn't built into much. Alex inherits very little established texture here, which means his failures around Joobin will be harder to attribute to specific gaps — she doesn't have a rich prior Yooyeon to compare against.

## Story Notes

- **The observer thread (Arc 3):** Joobin notices things about people before they present themselves. She prepares. She files. When Alex-as-Yooyeon enters her field she will register discrepancies — not through intimate familiarity with Yooyeon (the relationship was thin) but through the same habit she brings to everyone. She won't know what to do with what she notices, at least not immediately. She'll hold it. The English thread is parallel: Alex's native-level comfort in English around Joobin is the most specific slip she is positioned to catch, and she is positioned to catch it because she is the one person in the group whose English is good enough to read the fluency register accurately.

- **English as the first crack:** He relaxes into English with her because she can meet him there, and the relief of not managing himself is briefly stronger than the vigilance of passing as Yooyeon. Joobin notices the fluency is different. She files it alongside whatever else she has already observed. She doesn't raise it immediately.
