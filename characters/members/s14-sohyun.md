## Profile

- **MBTI:** INTJ — Original, independent, long-range thinker; high internal standards; develops clear private maps of situations. Her protectiveness toward SeoAh is INTJ care: not demonstrative, but decided. She identified someone who needed watching out for and does it.
- **Role in the group:** TBD — expand as arc develops.
- **Protective quality:** Takes on a caretaking role toward SeoAh, the youngest member of the group. The protectiveness is genuine, not performed — she looks out for her. Whether this extends to a general tendency to care for people who are newer or more vulnerable in the group, or whether it is specific to SeoAh, is TBD.
- **Relationship with Yooyeon (pre-swap):** Friends. Listed among Sohyun's real friendships within the group.

### Physical Appearance

- **Face:** Considered good-looking since she was young. Her features are androgynous — a mix of feminine and masculine characteristics that allows her to read as boyish or girlish depending on how she presents. This is publicly received well: WAVs have distinct preferences and respond to both versions of her, some preferring the masculine presentation, others the feminine. The reception is positive overall.
- **How she presents:** She doesn't fix to one register. In performances where the group covers or performs boy-group material, she leans into the masculine side — shorter hair styling, boyish dress, that direction. In other contexts she uses longer hair, more feminine makeup and outfits. She moves between these without tension in terms of how she performs them.
- **Private relationship to her appearance:** The androgynous quality is a mild insecurity — not a defining weight, not something that shapes her behavior or how she carries herself, but something that sits there. She has at times felt envious of members like Xinyu, who reads as conventionally model-like: tall, feminine-featured, well-proportioned. She knows she looks good. The occasional envy is not about doubting that — it is about a specific kind of look she doesn't have. She does not talk about this.

### Music Production

- **Background:** Sohyun produces music. She was involved in production work before debuting in tripleS and has continued producing since. It is a genuine creative practice, not an accessory to her idol identity.
- **Working style:** Works alone by default. Her process is self-contained — she does not naturally open the project up to others and does not need collaboration to function creatively. She is a strong lyricist and treats the lyric-writing process as private: she writes alone, and lyrics remain hers until they are fully cooked and part of something ready to share.
- **Openness to collaboration:** She is not closed off — if someone brings ideas that could genuinely contribute to a project, she will let them in. The invitation is earned by the quality of the idea, not extended by default. What she shares is the outer layer: direction, concept, structural input. The lyric layer stays private until the song is finished.
- **Sessions with Yooyeon:** Yooyeon had an interest in Sohyun's production work and participated in one or two sessions. Her role was in the directing and contribution layer — ideas, creative direction — not in writing. Sohyun let her in because she wasn't being asked to share the private part of the process. These sessions represent a specific texture in their friendship: Yooyeon was trusted with the work at a level most people are not, even if not the deepest level.
- **Sessions with Dahyun:** Dahyun has also participated, drawn in through her voice — steady, suitable for what Sohyun is making. The collaboration with Dahyun is different in kind from Yooyeon's: it is more functional, built around what Dahyun's voice contributes to the sound.
- **Private notes:** Sohyun writes notes — phone, whatever is nearby — that become raw material for songs. Some of what she processes in those notes does not make it into finished output. The private layer carries self-doubt: about her work, her appearance, her future. If she has written anything more intimate than that, it has not been made public and is not a subject this story will open.

### Core Psychology

- **The gap:** What the group sees is someone competent, self-contained, watchful. The private layer carries self-doubt — about her work, about how she looks, about where things are going. The INTJ surface does not advertise this gap. She is not performing confidence she doesn't have; she is simply not broadcasting the uncertainty. Both things are true at once.
- **Insecurity and output:** Her private notes are partly a processing space for the doubt. The finished song — whatever survives the process — is the distilled version, presentable and public. What stays in the notes stays there. She does not share this layer with anyone.

### Relationships

- **Xinyu (best friends, fanservice dynamic):** Best friends. They perform as a married couple in fanservice contexts — a named, established dynamic that the group and fans recognize. Sohyun participates in a shy way: she engages, but with a quality that reads as bashful rather than performative ease. Whether that shyness reflects actual feeling she is managing, or simply her natural register in affectionate contexts, is not established. Xinyu's feelings are real; Sohyun's reciprocation, if any, is unknown — to Xinyu, to the group, possibly to Sohyun herself. A quiet additional layer: Xinyu is exactly the kind of conventionally model-like that Sohyun occasionally envies. Her closest person is also, specifically, the one whose appearance she sometimes measures herself against. Neither of them knows this.
- **Yooyeon (friends):** A real friendship with a specific texture: Yooyeon participated in one or two of Sohyun's production sessions, contributing creative direction. Sohyun let her into a part of her work she doesn't open to most people. What else the friendship is built on is TBD.
- **Kotone (friends):** TBD — relationship noted, texture not yet established.
- **SeoAh (protective):** Sohyun watches out for her specifically. SeoAh is S23, one of the four newest members (Glow unit). The protectiveness may have formed from proximity and the instinct to look out for whoever is newest.

## Thematic Note

Sohyun sits at the center of something she cannot fully see: Xinyu's real feelings are channeled into their fanservice, which Sohyun receives as either warmth or something-more-than-warmth without a way to confirm which it is. Her shyness in participating means she is not simply brushing it off — she is doing something with it. What she is doing with it is the open question.

There is also a private asymmetry in the opposite direction: Sohyun occasionally envies something about Xinyu that Xinyu doesn't know she's being envied for. The two of them orbit each other with a great deal of feeling neither has named — different in kind, different in direction, but sitting in the same relationship.

## Role in the Reveal (Arc 4)

**Faction: Enemy.**

Her position is rooted in two distinct layers she cannot fully separate and would not try to articulate.

The first is the production sessions. She let Yooyeon into a part of her work she doesn't open to most people — the contributing and directing layer, not the lyric layer, but still specific trust, specifically placed. Finding out that trust was given to a stranger produces something cold. Not explosive. She doesn't make it loud. She simply closes.

The second layer is personal and unspoken, and she would not name it even if pressed. Her own mild insecurity about being read as androgynous — the private envy of the conventionally feminine, the way she moves between registers without fully owning either — gives the specific nature of the swap an added dimension she cannot account for. A man inhabiting and performing a woman's body, navigating gender presentation from the inside. It does not map directly onto her situation. But it rhymes with a territory she keeps quiet, and the resonance produces something she could not name and would not try to. The disgust has a personal dimension she herself may not recognize.

Xinyu enters the faction alongside her, following her reaction. When Xinyu eventually shifts — her ENTP curiosity reasserting, the borrowed position dissolving — Sohyun is left holding it alone. The cost is now personal rather than principled: the stance has put distance between her and the person she's closest to. An INTJ works things out on her own internal timeline, but the question of whether this position is worth what it is costing with Xinyu becomes harder to avoid. Her reckoning is not about Alex directly. It is about whether she is right.

Her resolution, when it comes, is fully her own. Not derivative of anyone else's journey.
