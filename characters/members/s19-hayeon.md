## Profile

### Personality

- **MBTI:** ENTP — Confirmed. Quick, outspoken, generates situations. The consent-checking before fanservice is ENTP self-awareness applied deliberately: she identified the dynamic, saw how it could go wrong, and built a framework. She doesn't operate by impulse. She operates with a structure she built herself, which happens to look like impulse from the outside.
- **Talkative — categorically, not subject-specifically:** Most people in the group become chatty about things they care about. Hayeon talks regardless. Any topic, any energy level, any room — the words arrive. She's not performing sociability; language is just how she processes the world in real time. Silences with her are rare and usually meaningful.
- **Strong self-love:** Has a specific, well-maintained relationship with her own appearance. Not vanity in the insecure sense — closer to a settled conviction that she looks good, maintained with genuine enjoyment rather than anxiety. She says it openly, without embarrassment, which reads either as charming or exhausting depending on who's listening.
- **Stage vs. off-stage contrast:** On stage she is chic — cool presence, controlled, the kind of performer who reads older than she is. Off stage she is immediately and completely herself: talking, eating, laughing, taking up space without calculation. The gap between the two is large enough that people who only know her from performance content tend to be surprised by her in person.
- **Internal weakness and indecisiveness:** The confidence is real but not total. Underneath it is someone who second-guesses, who struggles with decisions, who can be shaken more easily than the exterior suggests. She doesn't often let this show. When it does, it tends to come out sideways — quiet for a moment longer than usual, a deflection through humor, a joke where a real answer might have been expected.
- **Sentimental about the journey:** She moved from being a normal teenager in Anyang to being a tripleS member in a compressed timeframe, and she has not stopped noticing the distance between those two selves. She talks about it — what it was like before, what it's like now, the strangeness of the comparison. Not in a performative gratitude way. More like she genuinely finds it interesting that the same person contains both.
- **She teaches:** Has actively helped other members with fanservice and social confidence. Taught SeoAh (S23) things she knew. The teaching impulse is genuine — she likes watching someone figure out a thing she already understands.
- **Physical expressiveness:** Particularly drawn to arm-touching as a form of affection. Noted this openly (Weekly Idol, 2024). Not the only form of skinship she's comfortable with — but the one she reaches for naturally.
- **Other skills:** Draws portraits — good ones, made quickly. Has an unusual ability to capture a face or object and render it in the time most people would be still looking. High note range that surprised people ("like a dolphin" was the comparison used). The dolphin comparison stuck because it was both accurate and slightly undignified, which she found funny.

### Fanservice — The Practice and the Framework

- **How it works:** Hayeon generates fanservice naturally and has a reputation for it. She is good at it, she enjoys it, and she has thought clearly about the fact that not everyone does. Before initiating anything with any member, she asks first — quietly, off-camera, in an ordinary moment. Not a formal negotiation. A direct and considerate check. Members who aren't comfortable don't get pushed. The asking is not a joke. Everything that follows from it can look like one.
- **The "mission" framing:** She talks about this lightly — a self-aware joke about her own tendencies. The joke is real; the framework underneath it is also real.
- **The kissed list (as of Day 0):** Mayu, Dahyun, Xinyu, Soomin, Nakyoung, Chaeyeon. Non-lip moments with: Chaewon, Joobin, Hyerin. The remaining members are either not cleared, not yet gotten to, or in a conditional status.
- **With Yooyeon — conditional clearance:** Yooyeon would allow it in a specific staged context — a couple skit, a fanmeeting cosplay moment, something where the situation sets it up. Not casually, not on a whim. This clearance exists because Yooyeon gave it in a specific conversation, possibly framed as a light promise between them — the kind of thing that both parties understand the terms of and neither treats as a standing invitation. Hayeon knows the frame. She does not push outside it.

## Key Relationships

### Chaewon (S21) — "Mochiz"
The official pairing name for Hayeon and Chaewon is **Mochiz**. They are both 2007-born, both in Alphie, and have a friendship that has developed enough to generate its own name and documented moments — including a nose kiss, and a noted moment where Hayeon expressed wanting to bite Chaewon's nose, which Chaewon accepted in the spirit it was offered. Despite being placed in rival positions during Badge War Season 3, Chaewon visibly rooted for Hayeon in the final round.

The quality of this friendship is almost romantic. Neither of them names it as such. Whether the not-naming is deliberate management of something they both sense, or simply how it has always been and they have never interrogated it, is ambiguous — and that ambiguity may be load-bearing. Naming it might change something neither of them wants changed. They have not lived in the same haus.

### Yooyeon (S5) — pre-swap
The pre-swap relationship between Hayeon and Yooyeon is not a close friendship. It is warm, collegial, and carries the specific history of the conditional clearance conversation. Yooyeon is seven years older — a generational gap within the group that neither of them performs but that shapes the dynamic. The conditional clearance was given in a specific register: affectionate, low-stakes, the way you make a light promise to someone you trust. Hayeon took it seriously without treating it as weighty. Yooyeon gave it without treating it as permanent.

Outside that context, they occupy the same large group and get along easily. Hayeon is in the Zenith orbital unit; Yooyeon is Sun. Their daily orbits don't overlap much. They will be placed in the same tour unit (Alphie) in 2025, which is when the relationship would have developed real depth — had the swap not intervened.

*The specific texture of how often they interact one-on-one before Day 0 is thin. Not cold — just not close yet.*

### Zenith unit members — Jiwoo, Soomin, Yubin, Yeonji, Joobin
As leader, Hayeon holds a specific kind of responsibility toward this group. She was not voted in — she was drawn. Whether she fully settled into the role or still sometimes feels the randomness of it is TBD. More likely: she generates energy, keeps things from going stagnant, and trusts that the others can manage the pieces she's not watching.

## Story Notes

### The Pinky Promise Scene (Arc 3)

The Hayeon-Yooyeon conditional clearance was never a formal agreement — it was lighter than that. A joke, or something close to one. A moment between them, probably at a fanmeeting or group schedule, where the idea came up and Yooyeon agreed in the spirit of the moment. A pinky promise in the literal or informal sense. Both of them knew what it meant; neither treated it as a standing commitment. Hayeon filed it as: if the situation comes up, Yooyeon is okay with it.

Alex does not know this exists. There was no reason for him to find it — it wasn't written down, wasn't referenced anywhere he would have looked. When the moment comes — a cosplay setup, a fanmeeting skit, a camera angle that produced an expectation — it happens before he can position himself. The kiss lands. He did not see it coming.

Hayeon notices immediately that something is wrong. "Yooyeon's" reaction is not what she expected. It is not the reaction of someone who agreed to this. The wrongness is visible enough that Hayeon, who pays attention to people even when she is generating situations rather than reading them, registers it clearly.

She finds him in a private moment — not long after, probably the same day — and apologizes. Simply, directly, without making it larger than it needs to be. She says she thought it was still okay. She's sorry if it wasn't. She won't again without asking.

**What Alex does with this:** He doesn't know how to receive the apology. The apology is clean — uncomplicated, well-meant, offered without anything asked in return. He has no framework for that. He says something like "it's fine" because that's the only thing available to him. The thing is not entirely fine. He doesn't know how to say what the thing actually is, and even if he did, he wouldn't say it to her. He carries it for a bit. Not anger at Hayeon — she had every reason to think what she thought. Just the weight of not knowing what to do with the fact of it. The discomfort will sit with him longer than the incident itself warrants.

*Note: Hayeon is 16 at Day 0. She turns 17 in August 2024. Alex is 30 internally. This does not need to be the primary lens on his discomfort — the identity displacement and the blindside are sufficient — but it will exist underneath everything. He will be aware of it without wanting to think about it directly.*

### Hayeon as Alex's unexpected safety net (all other cases)
Hayeon will not initiate anything with "Yooyeon" beyond what has already been agreed to. Her framework prevents it. If the conditional clearance was context-specific and Alex doesn't replicate that context, she won't push. She will ask first. Alex doesn't know he has this protection, and she has no idea she's providing it.

### When Hayeon notices something is off with Alex (general — beyond the kiss)
She will notice. She pays attention to people even when she's doing the talking. The question is whether she treats the noticing as something to bring to "Yooyeon" directly, or whether she watches and waits. Given her ENTP directness, probably she brings it up. Probably gently, framed as checking in rather than accusing. Whether she connects it to anything larger before the formal reveal is TBD.
