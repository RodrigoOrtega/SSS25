## Profile

- **MBTI:** ISTJ — Quiet, thorough, dependable; takes responsibilities seriously; high standards. The ballet background fits: years of disciplined practice toward an exacting standard, in a world where the margin for error is physical and immediate. The composed, reliable surface is real. So is what breaks through it.
- **The dream and what it cost:** Ballet was her life for most of it — Seonhwa Arts Middle and High School (ballet major), then Hanyang University's dance and ballet program, training at Universal Ballet Academy (associated with one of South Korea's five professional ballet companies). She was enrolled at Hanyang when MODHAUS scouted her via Instagram in August 2023. She was not transitioning out of ballet; she was pulled mid-pursuit. The switch to idol training required her to build an entirely different skillset — performance register, fan interaction, the group dynamic — from near-zero, while competing for a debut slot in a group that already had 23 members.

  She was not the obvious pick. Jaden Jeong wrote afterward that she was not originally a favored candidate. In her final selection interview she asked him: *"Are you sure you won't regret it if you don't pick me?"* That question changed the outcome. It is not an anxious question. It is a challenge delivered directly to the person making the decision, by someone who knew exactly how the odds were arranged and chose to name the situation rather than hope. There is steel underneath the warmth — not hidden, just not the thing she leads with.

- **The arrival:** She has talked about what her parents' support meant to her, and about how much it means that so many people like her. This is not a publicity talking point. She knows what it took — the years in ballet, the pivot, the audition she wasn't supposed to win — and she knows who was alongside her for it. When people receive her warmly, she feels the full weight of that reception because she knows the full weight of what it took to get here. That is what the flustered quality is. Not shyness. Not naivety. Someone who has fought for something and genuinely feels it when they arrive.

- **Ballet technique:** The training is still in her body — posture, spatial awareness, the precision that years of classical technique embeds. Her competitive record is real: 2016 Silver Award at the Korea Professional Ballet Association competition; 2018 K-PROBA Grand Prize and Special Prize for Giselle (at approximately fourteen); 2021 Swan Lake pas de deux at a Universal Ballet Academy performance. Her official emoji is 🦢, swan imagery. The group uses "ballerina" as a light nickname; she has not been documented expressing complicated feelings about it in any direction. It is simply part of what she is.

- **The mimic:** She is independently identified by members as one of the group's best mimics — some consider her the best. She and Kotone carry this quality simultaneously, which is almost certainly part of what makes their closeness specific: they recognize the same attentiveness in each other. The mimicry, like Kotone's, is not a performance skill she deploys — it is evidence of close observation.

- **Fanservice:** Rarely initiates it. When other members bring it to her, she gets flustered — visibly, without performing it. She does not deny or redirect; she just gets there. The flustering is consistent with the broader picture: someone who genuinely feels reception rather than managing it. She participates in a visible flirtatious dynamic with Yubin specifically, which is the most consistent exception to her generally non-initiating stance.

- **The Counselling Center:** Members go to her with problems. The nickname is consistent across fan documentation without a clear origin moment. For an ISTJ: reliable, non-theatrical, contained. She does not make a moment of receiving what people bring her. She just receives it.

- **Horror:** She is afraid of horror content and does not perform bravery about it. After watching a scene involving a ghost emerging from under a bed, she changed her bed to a style with no gap underneath. She saw a problem and fixed it. This is a small, specific, characterizing thing — she takes her own fears seriously enough to act on them without embarrassment.

- **Everyday:** Her listed hobbies are diary writing, laying down, and cleaning. Diary writing is internal processing made external — consistent with someone who has a private register that the warm surface does not fully convey. Cleaning as a source of satisfaction is ISTJ order-orientation. "Laying down" is either deadpan self-inventory or genuinely her preferred low-stakes activity. She plays Minecraft. She is the only member of tripleS with a driver's license, which gives her a quiet structural role in the group's daily logistics.

- **Says what she observes:** When asked about her first impressions of the other Glow members, she said Chaewon "looked Japanese," SeoAh "looked really mature," and she thought Sullin was Korean. Candid, slightly incorrect (Chaewon is Korean), unedited. She reports what she actually perceived.

### Background

Born February 13, 2004 in Yongin, Gyeonggi-do. Her legal name is Ji Seoyeon (지서연); she uses the stage name Jiyeon to avoid confusion with S1 Seoyeon (Yoon Seoyeon). She is the only tripleS member whose stage name was chosen by fan vote — a Gravity event held April 6–7, 2024, with five options including a boy's name on the ballot (DoJoon). Jiyeon won with the largest share. The name was formed by removing the middle character from her legal name. She attended FLAT9 Dance & Vocal Academy during her trainee period, sharing that context with Dahyun (S10), Joobin (S18), and Hayeon (S19) — they were not strangers before ASSEMBLE24.

Both are born in 2004 — Jiyeon February 13, Kotone March 10, 26 days apart — making them age-mates in a group where cohort proximity matters. ## Story Notes

- The double-unit situation (Glow + Visionary Vision) makes Jiyeon one of the busiest members around the story's opening arc. She will be visible and in motion during a period when Alex is trying not to be noticed.
- The flustered quality is not separable from the steel. Both are real. She pushed through an audition she wasn't supposed to win, and she genuinely feels it when people receive her. These are the same person. For Alex, whose job is to perform an identity he doesn't have, encountering someone whose responses are this unmediated is worth noting — he will recognize authentic arrival because he is producing so little of it.
- The Counselling Center function: members go to her with problems. Alex will inherit this role adjacently — people may come to "Yooyeon" the way they always have, not knowing Yooyeon is not there. Jiyeon's version of this dynamic is quieter and more contained than Yooyeon's. The difference may register as a texture shift without immediately suggesting replacement.
- Jiwoo's discomfort around the Yubin–Jiyeon dynamic (per Jiwoo's profile) is a layer Jiyeon is unaware of. She is not doing anything wrong. She is simply caught in a triangle whose shape she cannot see.
- The ballet body. She still carries classical technique — posture, spatial awareness, the way someone moves who has trained that way for years. Alex does not. He is in Yooyeon's body, not Jiyeon's, but Jiyeon has ballet eyes and may notice things about how people move. The Kaede thread (reads bodies in rehearsal) and the Jiyeon thread (reads bodies through classical training) are parallel ways the Visionary Vision space becomes dangerous for Alex.
