## Profile

- **MBTI:** INFJ — Builds quiet internal maps of people and situations; reads underneath the surface rather than through it. Her empathy is structural, not sentimental: she understands how things work before she names how she feels about them.
- **Personality:** Has a composed, elegant exterior that routinely surprises people. She can present as calm and put-together, and then produce something erratic or childlike or genuinely funny that nobody saw coming. The humor is hers — not manufactured, not purely a byproduct of the language barrier, though the language gap adds texture to it. She finds the shape of what went wrong before anyone else names it, and sometimes that comes out as comedy and sometimes as a quiet read that arrives a beat ahead of everyone else.
- **Self-reliance:** Solves problems independently by default. Does not ask for help easily. This is not stubbornness — it is how she is wired. She prefers to carry the thing herself until she has a solution, then present the solution rather than the problem. This operates quietly enough that people around her may not notice how much she's working through on her own.
- **Background:** From Kanchanaburi originally, grew up in Bangkok. Attended DCT Family, a K-pop training and audition facilitator. Was offered a contract by High Up Entertainment at a training camp and declined, returning to Thailand instead. Auditioned for MODHAUS months later, also through DCT Family. That sequence matters: she chose this specifically. She could have taken an easier path and didn't. Her presence in tripleS is the result of a deliberate decision made twice.
- **Skills:** Horse riding is a documented special talent — something from before the idol path, not a training-center skill. Her dance background was minimal when she joined, and her prep time for "Girls Never Die" was short; she has closed that gap through visible work, with her "Inner Dance" performance receiving specific favorable notice. Vocally, she contributes a soft, gentle tone.
- **Languages:** Thai native. English fluent. Chinese capable. Korean in active progress — self-driven study, improving notably since debut. Four languages at varying levels of fluency, with the fifth (Korean) being the one she inhabits most of her daily life in. She has lived the experience of meaning something and not having the word for it, of reading around what isn't said because what is said doesn't quite fit. This is not a weakness she is working to eliminate. It has become part of how she moves.
- **Favorite character:** Harley Quinn. Not a casual choice — a character defined by duality, charm under pressure, and the gap between presentation and interior.

## Story Notes

- **The foreigner parallel:** Sullin knows — from lived experience — what it is to be inside a group without being fully of it yet. To be in the room while missing references everyone else shares. To be doing your best and still having things not land quite right. She knows what the gap between meaning and expression feels like from the inside. This gives her a specific lens on Alex's situation that most Korean members don't have access to: she recognizes the texture of not-quite-fitting because she has lived a version of it. This is not metaphor. It is structural. She does not need to be told to understand — she has her own map for it.
- **No resentment in the parallel:** The version of this idea that doesn't belong to Sullin is the comparative one — *she worked to earn her place; he didn't.* That's not how she reads the world. The version that does belong to her is experiential: she knows what that particular kind of displacement feels like, and she recognizes it. Full stop.
- **The English thread:** Alex is half-American and fluent in English. In proximity to Sullin (and Joobin), under enough pressure that he stops monitoring himself carefully, he may slip into English with a fluency that Yooyeon would not have had. Sullin would likely just be glad someone is keeping up with her. Joobin might notice the register is different. A minor exposure thread, but real.
- **Intelligence underneath:** The puzzle-solving moment with Hayeon is a data point. She is sharper than the language barrier might suggest to people who aren't paying attention. Her INFJ pattern-recognition means she may be one of the first in the undecided group to form an independent read — not through suspicion, but through mapping.
