## Profile

- **MBTI:** ENFP — Warmly enthusiastic and imaginative; makes connections faster than she can stop herself voicing them; processes the world by externalizing it. The associative, continuous talking is Ne running aloud — the connections arrive before she decides whether to voice them, and she voices them.
- **Personality:** The chattiest member, which is saying something in a group that includes Soomin. The distinction is in how the talking lands: Soomin's chattiness carries energy, warmth, physical presence — she fills a room. Yeonji's is different. She is not a ball of energy; she simply likes talking. The words come because the words come. She will describe a cookie she just ate, then rank every cookie she has ever had, then describe how she found out about each one, then recall who she was with when she first tried each, then follow that thread into a memory, then follow the memory into something else entirely — for several minutes, without a point, without needing one. She is not performing; she is just running. The content is not gossip or philosophy. It is the continuous, associative output of a person who processes the world by narrating it aloud.
- **Fanservice:** Does not participate in the physical fanservice common among some members. Appears to have adopted this in proximity to Kotone, who has a principled reason for opting out. Whether Kotone explained that reason to Yeonji, or whether Yeonji simply observed and followed the behavior without the reasoning, is not yet established. Yeonji does not make a point of it — she just doesn't engage in that register.

## Thematic Note

Yeonji's verbal style — associative, continuous, without destination — is the opposite of Alex's internal register. He processes slowly and in private; she externalizes everything as it arrives. In shared scenes, that gap will be felt. She is not asking him to match her; she just talks. But someone performing constantly and monitoring every response will find her volume difficult: she produces too much, too fast, and expects nothing particular back — which should be a relief but won't feel like one.
