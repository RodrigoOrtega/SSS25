# Hauses

## Overview

"Haus" is the members' term for the residential units they share. All 24 tripleS members live in hauses within the same building — a practical arrangement that keeps the group in close proximity and simplifies scheduling and management oversight.

The building is managed by MODHAUS. The arrangement has been in place since [TBD — when did members move into this building?]. The internal social dynamics within and between hauses are treated as the members' private business; management handles logistics but does not supervise how the members organize themselves.

## Member Assignment

Members are assigned to hauses by agency arrangement. The initial assignment logic is not explained to members — it is simply how the building was set up. Assignments are stable for extended periods.

The pre-reveal groupings follow a rough debut-cohort logic with practical cross-seeding — earlier members distributed across hauses, newer members clustered together. Spring's Japanese-member pairing (Kotone and Mayu) reflects the kind of practical grouping management would apply without a second thought. Winter's concentration of the newest members (S18–S24) is the most deliberate clustering.

> **Author note:** Post-reveal haus assignments do NOT correspond to orbital unit membership. The factions and their haus placements are determined by personality-based response to the reveal.

## Rotation System

Haus assignments rotate periodically — approximately every [TBD — X months]. Rotation is logged with management and treated as routine. The members decide among themselves when to rotate and how; changes are submitted through standard channels. Because rotation is normal and expected, management does not question it.

Seoyeon typically handles practical group-to-management communication, including haus rotation logistics. This becomes relevant in Arc 4.

## Pre-Reveal Haus Dynamics (Arcs 1–4)

### Spring haus (Floor 4)
Jiwoo (S3), Alex/Yooyeon (S5), Nakyoung (S7), Kotone (S11), Xinyu (S15), Mayu (S16)

The haus Alex wakes up in on Day 0. The most narratively loaded pre-reveal space.

- **Kotone and Mayu are already here.** Management's J-Line grouping puts the two people who will anchor the ally haus in Alex's immediate domestic environment. The discovery, the telling, and the early ally formation all happen within the same four walls. The secret never has to cross a floor.
- **Nakyoung** provides real reference points for how Yooyeon behaves in casual domestic settings, but her INFP instinct is to not press on others' inner lives. She will notice wrongness and give it room rather than push into it. Survivable for Alex in the short term — more dangerous as time accumulates.
- **Xinyu** has warm but not deep knowledge of Yooyeon — enough to register that things feel off, not enough to demand explanation. She becomes enemy post-reveal, which means three of Alex's six pre-reveal housemates end up in the enemy faction. The Spring haus contains the highest concentration of future enemies of any pre-reveal arrangement.
- **Jiwoo** is the deliberate choice over Soomin. She has a documented capacity to sit with observations she doesn't act on (the Yubin feeling). She won't broadcast half-formed suspicions through her social network. What she accumulates, she holds — until the group meeting forces it into the open.

Seoyeon (S1), Hyerin (S2), Yubin (S8), Kaede (S9), Sohyun (S14), Chaewon (S21)

Seoyeon's pre-reveal home. The most senior-weighted haus. Seoyeon's role as logistics manager means her haus functions as something close to an informal group hub.

After the reshuffle, Seoyeon has to leave Summer. The paperwork she files — the rotation she submits as routine — displaces her from her own haus. She moves into the enemy Autumn haus. The group's administrative anchor is now living among the people who want Alex gone.

### Autumn haus (Floor 3)
Chaeyeon (S4), Soomin (S6), Dahyun (S10), Yeonji (S12), Nien (S13), Lynn (S17)

EVOLution-heavy cluster: Chaeyeon, Dahyun, and Yeonji are all EVOLution members, with Soomin and Nien adding documented bilateral warmth (Legend pairing, Soomin + Yeonji; 999z connections, Nien). A naturally coherent haus with a lot of daily verbal energy.

### Winter haus (Floor 3)
Joobin (S18), Hayeon (S19), Shion (S20), Sullin (S22), SeoAh (S23), Jiyeon (S24)

The newest members, S18–S24. The most stable pre-to-post arrangement — Winter loses Sullin (to ally) and gains Nien and Lynn from Autumn; Hayeon moves to Middle A. Four members stay: Joobin, Shion, SeoAh, Jiyeon, forming the stable core of Middle B.

## Post-Reveal Reorganization (Arc 4)

Following the full-group reveal, the members reorganize haus assignments collectively, during or immediately after the group meeting. This is a self-organized decision — not management-directed. The reorganization is submitted to management by Seoyeon as routine. Management has no reason to look further.

Kotone and Mayu are the most vocal advocates for the arrangement that protects Alex. The reorganization is their first fully public commitment to his side — and the enemy faction will not forget it.

### Ally haus — order of commitment

The five ally members did not all commit simultaneously. The sequence matters because it reflects how the position formed — not as a bloc, but as individuals arriving at the same place through different routes:

1. **Mayu** — commits publicly at the group meeting ("I'll be responsible for him"). Her declaration is what makes the ally haus structurally real.
2. **Kotone** — seconds Mayu at the meeting. Her guilt about initiating the chain, and her prior understanding of Alex as victim, make her commitment immediate.
3. **Sullin** — commits within the meeting or immediately after. Her foreigner parallel gives her a ready framework: she recognizes displacement from the inside and it activates fast.
4. **Yeonji** — arrives within days. Her ENFP processing is loud at first (grief, disorientation), but once it has a shape, empathy follows. She gets there herself; the Kotone connection accelerates the arrival but does not determine it.
5. **Dahyun** — arrives last among the allies, after private internal processing. When she commits, it is settled and not provisional.

### Immediate night — the practice room

The haus reorganization settles over the days following the group meeting, not on the night itself. But Alex cannot return to the Sun haus — Xinyu (enemy) is already there. The same night as the meeting, Mayu moves him to the practice room in the basement. She stays with him. This is the first physical expression of what her "I'll be responsible" declaration means: not a speech, just her making a bed in the basement and sitting there.

Kotone is likely involved in the arrangement from the floor above, but Mayu acts because she is already Alex's haus member and the most immediate responsible party.

## Faction Definitions (Post-Reveal)

### Ally haus — 80 to 100 support

These members heard Alex's account and believed him. They understand the swap was not his choice. They extend sympathy to him as a person — not because they preferred him to Yooyeon, but because they refuse to abandon Yooyeon's body to despair, and because they found his situation genuinely unjust. Their support is not unconditional or uncomplicated. It is real.

The group as a whole learning to understand Alex, and whether they come to like him, is something that happens on each individual's terms — not as a collective arc. The ally haus is not a monolith; it is five people who arrived at a similar position through different routes.

Members: Mayu (S16, anchor), Kotone (S11, anchor), Sullin (S22), Yeonji (S12), Dahyun (S10).

### Enemy haus — 0 to 10 support

These members want nothing to do with Alex. The dehumanizing register — "that thing," or equivalent — is their shared language for him. This is not cartoonish cruelty. It is grief wearing the mask of contempt: hostility directed at what Alex represents (Yooyeon's absence, the occupation of her body and life) as much as at Alex himself.

The enemy haus is closed off not only to Alex but to the ally haus members. Supporting "the thing" is understood as choosing it over Yooyeon — which makes the allies secondary targets in the enemy faction's emotional logic. The middle members, who have not declared, are the only ones the enemy haus will receive.

Living inside the enemy haus reinforces the position. Six people who share a grief and a verdict validate each other daily. This is a structural reason — not just a personal one — that Seoyeon's arc out of the enemy position takes longer than anyone else's.

Members: Seoyeon (S1, anchor), Sohyun (S14), Xinyu (S15, first to shift — see below), Nakyoung (S7), Yubin (S8), Soomin (S6).

> **Xinyu exception:** Xinyu begins in the enemy haus but is the first member to shift. She does not start there by mistake — her initial position is genuine. But living surrounded by five people who have hardened around a shared verdict means that when her doubt begins, she carries it privately, against the current of her own living situation. Her shift is more costly and more significant for it. She is the first crack in the enemy haus's cohesion.

### Middle hauses — 11 to 79 support

The majority of the group. These members are neither supporters nor enemies — they are genuinely uncertain, processing on their own terms. They have not declared a position, and they are trusted by neither side fully, which makes them the only ones capable of serving as a communication bridge between the ally and enemy hauses.

The two middle hauses are not identical in internal composition. They cluster at different points within the 11–79 range without this being by design.

**Middle A:** Hyerin (S2), Jiwoo (S3), Chaeyeon (S4), Kaede (S9), Hayeon (S19), Chaewon (S21). Key bonds: Jiwoo↔Chaeyeon (former roommates, very close), Hayeon↔Chaewon (Mochiz, near-romantic), Kaede↔Chaeyeon (documented pull toward closeness), Hyerin in Hayeon's daily circle.

**Middle B:** Nien (S13), Lynn (S17), Joobin (S18), Shion (S20), SeoAh (S23), Jiyeon (S24). Key bonds: Lynn↔Shion (Muhaez, closest pairing), Lynn↔Jiyeon (Moon unit), Joobin↔Jiyeon (FLAT9 pre-debut), SeoAh↔Joobin (No Same Age Line), SeoAh↔Shion (MOD Forest roommates), Nien↔SeoAh (breaking-things/screaming recurring pattern). The most internally connected of the four hauses.

Several Middle B members are documented as drifting toward ally over time: Sullin (already in ally), SeoAh (ESTP pragmatism resolves fast), Lynn (gives without conditions). The middle hauses are not static.

## Group Dynamic Post-Reorganization

- The enemy haus's self-reinforcement slows individual movement out of it. Seoyeon, as anchor, is the last to shift.
- The ally haus is not immune to pressure — being openly associated with Alex comes at social cost within the broader group.
- Alex's behavior post-reveal carries weight. He cannot control outcomes, but how he carries himself — whether he shows care for Yooyeon, whether he is honest, whether he treats members as people rather than a problem to manage — will affect how individuals in the middle move over time. He has agency here, even if it doesn't feel like it.
- The middle members are not passive. Their role as bridge is load-bearing for the story's resolution. They are crucial precisely because they have not committed.
